june the 19th, 2018

These days, everything is: difficult to define. Pockets of pseudo-marital bliss punctuate the stormy weather. I turn 29 tomorrow.

-x-

These days, everything is: constantly changing. Growth is on the horizon, personal and otherwise. The year is halfway over & it's been eventful, surely, and tumultuous on occasion, but mostly it's been nurturing. I've moved into a new apartment & two bundles of sweet dried flowers decorate my workspace. I'm writing, presently, and I've been reading, presently.

A new friend, who owns a bookstore, sold me an entire (used) 1923 Yale Library collection of Shakespeare (40 books) for $10. I'm enthused. I finished reading King Henry The Sixth (Part Two) yesterday and Julius Caesar the month prior.

I have ambitions to be more forthcoming about my daily writing: I intend to share it, here, on my blog, with the world, every day, if only to make myself accountable for something.

Already I'm dreading words like 'mundane' and 'quotidian.'

-x-

I'm failing to find the balance between interpersonal and private/professional. What I mean is: sometimes, I live as if I exist in a vacuum. I'm able to withdraw into myself so completely, at times, that I no longer see or hear the person next to me. Sometimes, it's important for me to spend time shrouded in my bubble so that I'm able to focus on the words at hand.

About those sweet dried flowers — mauve blush roses mixed with golden-white ones, deep blue asters accented among them — they were a gift for B———, my love. The perks of a shared apartment include living with the gifts we buy for one another.

I feel as though I've been 29 for years. In fact, I'm only 28, but sometime after 24 everything started to blur together. Time moves so fast, anymore, that I'm amazed every time I glimpse the calendar. If I don't act now: when will I?
